“TalkTutor” Analyze speech implements of children by analyzing a dialogue.

Literature Survey

Speech and language development holds immense importance as a pivotal milestone in a child's growth, significantly influencing their cognitive and social progress. The key to this developmental journey often lies in the communication shared between a child and their primary caregiver, frequently the mother. However, it's important to recognize that some children may face challenges in their speech development, which can manifest as speech impairments, potentially impeding their ability to effectively convey thoughts and emotions. Detecting and addressing these impairments early on are essential for ensuring a child's successful communication development.



Research Gap

While there has been notable progress in the development of speech recognition technology and its application in speech analysis, there remains a research gap in the specific area of using speech recognition for the comparative analysis of mother-child speech to identify potential speech impairments in children. Existing literature predominantly concentrates on adult speech recognition and analysis, with a relatively limited emphasis on the unique challenges posed by child speech. Children's speech differs significantly from adult speech in terms of pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammatical structures. As a result, there is a need for dedicated research that tailors speech recognition systems to accurately transcribe and analyze child speech, particularly in a comparative context with maternal speech. While some research has explored the analysis of either maternal or child speech separately, there is a lack of comprehensive studies that integrate the speech of both the mother and child for direct comparison.



Research Problem

In an era marked by rapid technological advancements and an escalating demand for remote healthcare solutions, a pressing issue arises: the timely and precise identification of speech impairments in children. These impairments, encompassing challenges related to articulation, pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammatical development, represent a pivotal aspect of a child's linguistic journey and overall well-being. However, despite these crucial considerations, there remains a significant gap in addressing this matter effectively. The core of this problem stems from the absence of accessible and efficient tools that empower parents, particularly mothers who often play a central role in a child's early linguistic development, to actively engage in monitoring their child's speech development from the comfort of their own homes. The traditional path of diagnosing speech impairments often necessitates in-person visits to specialists, potentially delaying intervention and placing additional burdens on both parents and healthcare systems.



Main Objective

The main objective of project is the develop a decision supporting tool to identify speech impairments of children at the age between 3 – 5 by analysing a dialog/conversation voice record between child and parent or the child and the teacher. By speech diarization the system will analyse who is the parent and who is the child. Based to the child voice system gives the result on speech ability.
